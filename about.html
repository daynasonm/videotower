Video Tower

An interactive sculpture built from Video Libraries.

This project explores overstimulation through a towering structure with sources drawn from the local video archives and the NASA Image and Video Library. The tower functions as a dense archive of visual content, continuously shifting through the material to evoke the endless stream of media that surrounds us. The user can influence the system by controlling what content appears on the tower and how the tower moves, turning it into a reflection of their own choices, interests, and habits.

Orbiting around this tower is a reflective sphere that contains a distorted live image of the user. This sphere represents human consciousness: always present, always circling the overwhelming body of content. While the user can manipulate the tower itself, they cannot control the orbit of the sphere. Its motion remains fixed, suggesting that even when we feel agency over the media we consume, we are still bound within a larger system that keeps us rotating around it. In this way, the project frames overstimulation as a form something inescapable, where the self becomes trapped in a loop of endless visual consumption.

Built with Three.js and MediaPipe FaceMesh.
